# Devpost Submission Draft

## Project Name

DayBridge

## Elevator Pitch

DayBridge is an accessible daily support planner for older adults living independently and the family, neighbors, aides, and volunteers who help them. It turns scattered reminders, appointments, errands, medication routines, and check-ins into one plain-language day board.

## Inspiration

The Grizzly Hacks III prompt asks us to build for a group of people whose daily life looks very different from ours. For many seniors, the hard part of the day is not one big task. It is the chain of small handoffs: remembering the appointment, finding the insurance card, taking medication as prescribed, confirming the ride, asking for help, and letting family know everything is okay.

## What It Does

- Creates a readable daily board for routines, appointments, errands, medication reminders, and calls.
- Uses AI to break a care need into checkpoints and daily tasks.
- Gives caregivers gentle status signals so they can help without hovering.
- Keeps safety boundaries clear: reminders and coordination only, not medical advice.

## Who It Is For

Older adults who live independently, especially seniors who coordinate daily life across sticky notes, phone calls, text threads, calendars, and medical portals.

## The Problem

Existing productivity apps are usually built for younger workers. They assume tiny controls, fast typing, solo use, and abstract task language. Seniors often need a tool that is more concrete, more readable, and easier to share with trusted helpers.

## Why It Matters

Small misses can threaten confidence, independence, and peace of mind. DayBridge helps keep the senior in charge while making support easier for the care circle.

## Built With

React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind CSS, Convex, Convex Auth, Framer Motion, and Lucide React.
